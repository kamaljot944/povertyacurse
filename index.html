Why Poverty is a curse

Poverty traps families in a cycle of struggle, affecting health, education, and opportunities. It stifles potential and perpetuates inequality. Understanding the profound impact of poverty is the first step toward creating meaningful change.

Limited Access to Basic Needs

Poverty restricts access to essential resources like food, clean water, shelter, and healthcare, leading to malnutrition, poor health, and a higher mortality rate.

Lack of Education

People in poverty often cannot afford education, perpetuating a cycle of illiteracy and limiting opportunities for personal and professional development.

Poor Health Outcomes

Poverty is associated with higher rates of chronic illnesses, mental health issues, and inadequate healthcare, exacerbating suffering and reducing life expectancy.
